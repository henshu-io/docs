This guide walks you through editing a podcast episode from start to finish. By the end, you'll have a finished audio file ready to publish.

Before you start, make sure you've created a show and an episode from the dashboard.

## Upload your audio

1. In the editor, click **Upload** or drag and drop your audio files into the episode
2. Each file card will show **Processing...** while Henshu prepares it for editing
3. Once processing finishes, the file is ready to edit. Enhancement and transcription continue in the background.

For more details, see [Uploading Audio](/creating/uploading-audio).

## Arrange your content

Use sections and audio blocks to structure your episode:

- Add sections to divide your episode into parts (e.g., Intro, Main Content, Outro)
- Drag audio blocks between sections to reorganize
- Add pause blocks to insert silence between audio
- Cut or mute unwanted parts by selecting regions on the waveform

<video src="/images/arrange-your-content.mp4" autoPlay muted loop playsInline style={{ width: '100%', borderRadius: '8px' }} />

For more details, see [Organizing Sections and Blocks](/editing/organizing).

## Transcribe and edit with text (optional)

Transcription happens automatically after upload. Once it's ready, you can edit by reading your audio as text:

1. Open the transcript panel for an audio block
2. Read the transcript and select any words you want to cut or mute
3. The waveform updates automatically to reflect your text selections

[screenshot: Transcript panel with text selected for cutting]

For more details, see [Editing Your Audio](/editing/editing-audio).

## Check your AI enhancement (optional)

Henshu automatically enhances your audio when you upload it, applying noise reduction, mouth noise removal, and volume leveling. Enhancement is off by default. Toggle it on for voice recordings to get cleaner audio.

If any of your audio blocks contain non-voice content (music, jingles, sound effects), leave enhancement off to avoid artifacts.

[screenshot: Audio block showing the enhancement toggle]

For more details, see [AI Audio Enhancement](/editing/ai-enhancement).

## Add background music (optional)

1. Select a section in the editor
2. Open the BGM panel and browse the music library
3. Click a track to assign it to the section
4. Adjust volume, ducking, and fade settings as needed

[screenshot: BGM panel with track assigned to a section]

For more details, see [Adding Background Music](/editing/background-music).

## Preview your episode

Before generating, use the playback controls at the bottom of the editor to preview how your episode will sound. Playback respects all your edits (cuts, mutes) and plays sections and blocks in order, including any background music. The minimap at the bottom lets you jump between sections.

## Generate your episode

When you're happy with your edits:

1. Click **Export** in the top-right corner
2. Click **Assemble Episode** in the dialog
3. Henshu assembles all your sections, audio blocks, edits, and background music into a single file
4. Wait for processing to complete

[screenshot: Export dialog with Assemble Episode button]

For more details, see [Generating Your Episode](/publishing/generating).

## Download the final file

Once generation is complete:

1. Click **Download** to get your finished audio file
2. Upload it to your podcast hosting platform, or share it directly

[screenshot: Download button on generated episode]

For more details, see [Downloading & Sharing](/publishing/downloading).
