This page defines the key concepts and terms used throughout Henshu. If you encounter an unfamiliar term anywhere in the docs, you'll find its definition here.

## Block-Based Editing

Henshu uses a **block-based editing** model — instead of editing audio on a single continuous timeline, you organize your episode as a stack of named blocks inside sections. This is the core idea behind Henshu's editor and shapes how everything else works.

In a traditional audio editor, a 45-minute interview is one long waveform. Rearranging means tedious cut-and-paste. In Henshu, the same interview is a set of named sections — "Intro," "Interview," "Outro" — each containing audio blocks you can drag to reorder, edit independently, and assign music to separately.

Block-based editing sits above the waveform — when you need fine-grained control, the waveform is right there inside each block. But you start from structure and meaning, not from raw audio.

For more, see [Welcome to Henshu](/getting-started/welcome#what-is-block-based-editing).

## Episode Structure

Henshu organizes your podcast content in a hierarchy:

```
Show → Episode → Section → Audio Block
```

### Shows

A **show** is your podcast series — the top-level container for all your episodes. Think of it as "My Interview Podcast" or "Weekly Tech News." You create shows from the dashboard, and each show has its own settings, [background music library](/editing/background-music), and [show notes template](/publishing/show-notes).

### Episodes

An **episode** is a single installment of your show. Each episode contains one or more sections that you arrange and edit to create your final podcast file. Episodes have a status: **editing** (in progress) or **generated** (final file created).

### Sections

A **section** is a named container within an episode — for example, "Intro," "Interview," or "Outro." Sections are the top-level building blocks of your episode. You can drag sections to reorder them, and each section can have its own [background music (BGM)](/editing/background-music) with independent volume, ducking, and fade settings.

### Audio Blocks

An **audio block** lives inside a section and holds your actual audio content. This is where you edit — trimming, cutting, muting, and enhancing individual recordings. Audio blocks within a section play **sequentially** — one after another.

### Break Blocks

A **break block** is a special type of block that inserts silence for a specified duration. Use break blocks to control pacing and rhythm between audio blocks — like a pause between an intro and the main content.

## Editing Concepts

### Cuts and Mutes

Also known as: edits, trims

- A **cut** removes a portion of audio from playback — the audio before and after the cut plays seamlessly
- A **mute** silences a portion of audio — the duration is preserved but the sound is replaced with silence

You can create cuts and mutes either by selecting regions on the [waveform editor](/editing/audio-editor) or by selecting text in the [transcript](/editing/transcripts).

### Transcription

Also known as: transcript

**Transcription** is the process of converting your audio file into text using AI. Once transcribed, you can read your recording as text and make edits by selecting words — this is called [text-based editing](/editing/transcripts). Henshu uses AI-powered transcription with word-level timestamps so that text selections map precisely to audio regions.

### Enhancement

Also known as: audio cleanup, noise reduction, mastering

**Enhancement** is Henshu's one-click [AI audio processing](/editing/ai-enhancement) that improves your recording quality. It includes noise reduction and volume leveling. Enhanced audio files are marked with a gold badge in the editor.

## Music Concepts

### BGM (Background Music)

**BGM** stands for background music. Henshu provides a [music library](/editing/background-music) that you can browse and assign to individual sections of your episode. Each section can have its own BGM track with independent settings. Because BGM is per-section, your intro can have upbeat music while your interview has subtle ambient sound.

### Ducking

**Ducking** automatically lowers the background music volume when voice audio is playing, then brings it back up during pauses. This ensures your voice is always clearly audible over the music.

### Smart Loop

A **smart loop** is a BGM play mode that uses configured cue points to create seamless music loops with crossfade transitions. Unlike a basic loop (which restarts from the beginning), a smart loop crossfades at natural transition points in the music for a more polished sound.

BGM play modes:
- **Once** — play the track once and stop
- **Loop** — repeat from the beginning when the track ends
- **Smart loop** — crossfade loop using configured cue points

## Publishing Concepts

### Generate (Episode Generation)

Also known as: export, finalize, render

**Generating** an episode is the process of assembling all your sections, audio blocks, BGM, and edits into a single final audio file. This is the last step before you download and publish your podcast. See [Generating Your Episode](/publishing/generating) for details.

### Show Notes

**Show notes** are the companion text for your episode — a summary, highlights, and timestamped chapters. Henshu can [generate show notes automatically](/publishing/show-notes) from your transcripts using AI. You can also add static sections like credits, guest profiles, and subscribe links using a customizable template that applies across all episodes in a show.
