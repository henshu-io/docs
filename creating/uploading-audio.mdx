Upload your audio recordings to an episode to start editing. Henshu processes uploaded files automatically after upload.

## How do I upload audio?

1. Open an episode in the editor
2. Either **drag and drop** audio files into the editor, or click the **Upload** button and select files from your computer
3. Your files begin uploading, and you'll see a progress indicator
4. Once uploaded, the audio file card shows **Processing...**
5. When processing finishes, the file is ready to edit

[screenshot: Upload area in the editor with drag-and-drop zone]

You can upload multiple files at once. Large files are automatically handled with multipart upload.

## What happens after upload?

After you upload a file, you'll see a series of status indicators on the audio file card:

1. **Processing...** converts the file to a standard audio format for consistent editing. You need to wait for this to finish before editing.
2. **Enhancing...** runs the [AI enhancement pipeline](/editing/ai-enhancement) (noise reduction, mouth noise removal, tone optimization, volume leveling). You can start editing while this runs.
3. **Transcribing...** generates a [transcript](/editing/transcripts) of the audio. You can start editing while this runs.

Enhancement and transcription happen in the background. You don't need to wait for them to finish before arranging blocks, making cuts, or doing other edits.

## Supported formats

Henshu accepts common audio formats including WAV, MP3, M4A, AAC, FLAC, and OGG. Files are converted to a standard format during processing, so the original format doesn't affect editing quality.

## Important details

- Uploaded files appear in the **Files** tab. From there, you drag them into your desired audio blocks.
- You can upload while other files are still processing.

<Note>
  **Free plan limit:** Your account has a lifetime upload quota. To check your remaining quota or learn about plan limits, see [Plans & Quotas](/account/plans-and-quotas).

## Common Issues

**Upload seems stuck or isn't progressing**
Check your internet connection. Very large files take longer to upload. If the upload fails, try again.

**"Quota exceeded" error**
You've reached your plan's upload limit. See [Plans & Quotas](/account/plans-and-quotas) for details on your plan's limits and how to upgrade.

**My file format isn't accepted**
Make sure your file is a supported audio format (WAV, MP3, M4A, AAC, FLAC, OGG). Video files and non-audio formats are not supported.

**The file still says "Processing..."**
Processing can take a few seconds depending on file size. If it doesn't finish after a minute, try refreshing the page. Your file is safely stored and processing will continue.
